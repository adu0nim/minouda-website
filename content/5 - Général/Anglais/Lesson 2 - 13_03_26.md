**HOMEWORK FOR NEXT WEEK:**




Voici deux questions que tu peux poser aux autres étudiants :
**Past Simple :**
"What did you do last weekend?"
"Did you watch a movie yesterday?"
"Where did you go on your last vacation?"
"What did you eat for breakfast this morning?"
"How did you learn to ride a bike?"

**Present Perfect :**
"Have you ever traveled to another country?"
"Have you ever tried sushi?"
"How many books have you read this year?"
"Have you finished your homework yet?"
"Have you visited Paris before?"

1. Répondre au Past Simple
Structure :

✅ Sujet + verbe au prétérit (V-ed ou forme irrégulière) + complément

❌ Pas de "did" dans la réponse courte (sauf pour "Yes, I did" / "No, I didn’t")
Exemples :


Question : "Did you watch a movie yesterday?"

Réponse courte : "Yes, I did." / "No, I didn’t."
Réponse détaillée : "Yes, I watched ‘Inception’ with my friends. It was amazing!"


Question : "Where did you go on your last vacation?"

Réponse : "I went to Spain last summer. I visited Barcelona and Madrid."


Question : "What did you eat for breakfast?"

Réponse : "I ate toast and drank orange juice."

À éviter :

❌ "Yes, I watched." (Il manque "did" pour la réponse courte.)
❌ "I gone to Spain." (Mauvais usage du verbe irrégulier : "went" et non "gone".)


2. Répondre au Present Perfect
Structure :

✅ Sujet + have/has + participe passé (V-ed ou forme irrégulière) + complément

✅ Utilise "ever" ou "never" pour les expériences :

"Yes, I have." / "No, I haven’t."
"Yes, I’ve tried sushi before." / "No, I’ve never been to Paris."
Exemples :


Question : "Have you ever tried sushi?"

Réponse courte : "Yes, I have." / "No, I haven’t."
Réponse détaillée : "Yes, I’ve tried sushi several times! My favorite is salmon nigiri."


Question : "Have you finished your homework?"

Réponse : "No, I haven’t finished it yet. I’ll do it after dinner."


Question : "How many books have you read this year?"

Réponse : "I’ve read five books so far!"


À éviter :

❌ "Yes, I tried." (Il manque "have/has".)
❌ "I went to Paris." (Si la question est au present perfect, la réponse doit l’être aussi : "I’ve been to Paris.")
